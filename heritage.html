Nekemte city




Heritage site

Located in Nekemt town, the capital of East Wollega zone, Kumsa Moroda Palace represents one of the most important heritage sites in Oromia. Built by Greeks in 1870, it was the residence of former local kings from the end of the 19th to the middle of the 20th century. This tourist attraction sits at a distance of 318 kilometers to the west of Addis Ababa. Built by the King of Wollega in the 1870s and served as residence to the prominent Worra Bekere family until they were hauled off to Addis Ababa during the Derg. Kumsa Moroda was the third and last Moti, or ruler, of the Wollega kingdom also known as the Leqa Nekemte. His father was Moti Moroda Bakare
